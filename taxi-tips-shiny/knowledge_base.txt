CHUNK_01_PROJECT_OVERVIEW
This is an MSDS 6306 final project titled "Predicting NYC Yellow Taxi Tips". 
The dual goals are: (1) build a linear regression model to predict tip_amount that achieves RMSE ≤ 2 and MAE ≤ 1.5, and (2) develop at least two meaningful, non-trivial insights into when, why, and how much riders tip in NYC Yellow Taxis. 
We study only Yellow Taxi data for one full calendar year, loaded via arrow::read_parquet(). A focused subset (e.g., specific zones or times) is used when it improves insight quality and model performance. The project follows the data science pipeline: Import, Tidy, Transform, Visualize, Model, Communicate.

CHUNK_02_DATA_AND_SCOPE
Data source: https://www.nyc.gov/site/tlc/about/tlc-trip-record-data.page (Yellow Taxi trip records in Parquet format). 
We use one full calendar year of Yellow Taxi data only. 
No minimum sample size; quality of insights and model matters more than quantity. 
Tip-related variables are carefully defined and cleaned (tip_amount is the target). 
Analysis may focus on subsets such as Manhattan zones, credit-card trips, or specific times of day to produce stronger insights.

CHUNK_03_INSIGHTS_REQUIREMENT
The project requires at least two high-quality insights on tipping behavior. 
Insights must go beyond description: explain when tips are higher/lower, where they differ across zones, how trip characteristics relate to tipping (and whether relationships depend on other factors), and how payment or fare patterns associate with tip levels. 
Each insight is supported by careful analysis, visualizations, and thoughtful interpretation of why the pattern matters.

CHUNK_04_MODEL_REQUIREMENTS
We must build a linear regression model (methods limited to those studied in MSDS 6306) to predict tip_amount. 
The model must meet: RMSE ≤ 2 and MAE ≤ 1.5 on appropriate evaluation. 
We address linear regression assumptions (linearity, residual normality, homoscedasticity, independence, multicollinearity via VIF), handle outliers, perform feature engineering (e.g., interactions, logs, time bins, fare_per_mile), and document variable selection (often via EDA).

CHUNK_05_SHINY_APP_REQUIREMENTS
We built an RShiny dashboard with: clear professional design, interactive visualizations and controls (filters for zones, time, payment type, etc.), display of analysis and model results, and a natural language question-answering feature powered by RAG using Hugging Face as the LLM. 
The RAG component answers questions about tipping patterns, model interpretation, differences across zones/times, and summaries of findings, grounded in our actual project materials.

CHUNK_08_CURRENT_INSIGHTS_DRAFT
Insight 1 (in development): Tips tend to be significantly higher for credit-card payments during evening rush hours, especially in Manhattan entertainment zones. This may reflect rider satisfaction, social norms, or convenience of digital tipping.
Insight 2 (in development): There is a non-linear relationship between trip distance/fare_amount and tip amount that is moderated by passenger_count and time of day. Longer trips with higher fares per mile show different tipping behavior depending on the number of passengers and whether the trip occurs during peak vs off-peak hours.

CHUNK_09_MODEL_SUMMARY_PLACEHOLDER
Our linear regression model predicts tip_amount using features such as trip_distance, fare_amount (or fare_per_mile), passenger_count, pickup_hour, pickup_dow, airport indicator, payment_type, and selected interactions/logs. 
Feature engineering and variable selection were guided by EDA. 
Diagnostics (residual plots, Q-Q, VIF) were checked and assumptions addressed where possible. 
Current performance targets: RMSE ≤ 2, MAE ≤ 1.5 (final values saved in model.rds).
